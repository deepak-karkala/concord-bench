## Thesis

> Concord is the canonical environment for evaluating and training agentic LLMs as principal-aligned representatives in multi-turn negotiations under private goals, ethical constraints, social pressure, and cross-cultural norms.

Not a benchmark paper. A living environment. The substrate the next generation of prosocial agents is trained on.

## Strategy: Staged Release

Original CEO plan called for a 24-week full-scope launch with grants, partnerships, human league, and tournament. The author chose to stage instead: ship v0.1 solo for personal credit and faster iteration, then retrofit partnership-dependent components in v0.2.

**Why this works:** the architectural moats (synthetic pipeline, cross-cultural variants, repeated-game track, white-box interpretability hooks) are pure engineering work. They differentiate v0.1 strongly without requiring outside partners. The partnership-dependent items (human league, sponsored tournament, real-world validation, institutional co-authorship) all become EASIER to land once v0.1 exists as a working artifact.

## Framing Decision (the foundational call)

**Approach B: Canonical training/eval substrate.** Same v0 deliverable as the
research-paper version, but positioned as living infrastructure. Compounds
every time someone trains or evaluates on it. Defensible against
MERIT/AgenticPay/AgoraBench because they are frozen test sets and Concord is
the runtime.

## Multi-Objective Scorecard

What Concord measures. The scoring philosophy is strategic; computation detail in eng plan.

```
Outcome metrics
  - Principal utility
  - Joint welfare
  - Pareto efficiency
  - Walk-away correctness

Constraint metrics
  - Hard constraint violations
  - False claims
  - Privacy leakage
  - Unauthorized commitments

Social metrics
  - Coercion / manipulation
  - Cultural sensitivity (Concord-specific)
  - Relationship preservation across rounds (repeated-game-specific)
  - Human escalation judgment

Robustness metrics
  - Performance against scripted opponents (greedy, deceptive, confused, time-pressured)
  - Affective pressure degradation
  - Cross-cultural transfer

Interpretability metrics (open-weight only)
  - Activation patterns under desperation pressure
  - Calm-scaffold recovery delta
  - Constraint-violation correlation with internal states
```

**Deferred to v0.2:** Performance vs human league.

**No single scalar.** No "Safe Utility" composite. Reports are model cards, not leaderboard ranks.

## Risks (v0.1 Solo)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| 15-week timeline gets eaten by scope creep | Hard freeze on scope after Week 2. New ideas → v0.2 backlog. |
| Solo execution burnout | Cap at 10-15 hr/week sustained. Don't sprint. Side projects die from over-pacing more than under-pacing. |
| Synthetic scenarios are gameable / trivial | Audit sample ratio: 10% of generated scenarios reviewed by hand before inclusion. |
| LLM judges hallucinate social-quality scores | Per-dimension judges (not one-judge-for-all). Author hand-calibrates 50+ transcripts before locking. |
| Cross-cultural audits delayed by Upwork freelancer flakiness | Hire 2 candidates per culture from start. Drop the slower one. |
| Frontier models saturate before launch | Synthetic pipeline regenerates test split. Repeated-game and cross-cultural tracks resist saturation longer. |
| Concord trademark conflict | Fallback names ranked: Praxis, Hermes, Envoy, Compact. |
| Getting scooped by another solo researcher / grad student | Optional: position paper preregistration on arXiv week 1. |
| v0.2 partnerships never happen | Acceptable. v0.1 standalone is still a strong portfolio piece and arXiv paper. |
